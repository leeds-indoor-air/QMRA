Discrete event system QMRA model
Reservoirs

\[ d_i \frac{dC_i}{dt} = \sum_{j \neq i}^n m_{ji} C_j + \sum_{j \neq i}^n \beta_{ji} C_j - \sum_{k \neq i}^n \beta_{ik} C_i - Q_i C_i - \gamma_i C_i \]

where

  1. \( \mathbf{d} = (d_1, \ldots, d_n)\) are the resservoir dimensions (volumes or areas). ''Reservoirs'' such as pulmonary and mucous membrane exposures, will have nominal dimension 1 unit.
  2. \( \mathbf{C} = (C_1, \ldots, C_n)\) are concentrations (pathogen units / unit area or volume). For an infector, this is viral load, i.e., pathogen units per unit volume of resp. fluid, for an air zone, pathogen units per unit volume of air, for a surface, pathogen units per unit area, etc.
  3. \( (m_{ij}) \) is the source matrix . E.g., infector \(i\) breathing into an air zone \(j\) with \(m_{ij}\) being the volume of resp.fluid exhaled by infector, per unit time and \(C_i\) is the viral load in pathogen units per volume. Another example, a susceptible person \(j\) is exposed to air in zone \( i\) and \(m_{ij}\) is their pulmonary rate
  4. \( (\beta_{ij}) \) is the mixing matrix whose terms are the volume rates of mixing between reservoirs. E.g., air mixing between zones and droplet deposition from air onto surfaces
  5. \(Q_i\) and \(\gamma_i\) are sink terms , resp. rates of ventilation and pathogen decay from reservoir \(i\).

I.e.,

\[ \frac{d\mathbf{C}}{dt} = D^{-1} X \mathbf{C} \]

where \(D = \text{diag}(d_1, \ldots, d_n)\) and

\[ X_{ij} = \left\{ \begin{array}{rl} \beta_{ji} + m_{ji} &i \neq j\\ -\sum_{k \neq i} \beta_{ik} - Q_i - \gamma_i & i = j\\ \end{array} \right. \]
