\hypertarget{group__reservoir}{}\doxysection{Reservoirs}
\label{group__reservoir}\index{Reservoirs@{Reservoirs}}
\[ d_i \frac{dC_i}{dt} = \sum_{j \neq i}^n m_{ji} C_j + \sum_{j \neq i}^n \beta_{ji} C_j - \sum_{k \neq i}^n \beta_{ik} C_i - Q_i C_i - \gamma_i C_i \]

where


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item $ \mathbf{d} = (d_1, \ldots, d_n)$ are the {\bfseries{ resservoir dimensions }} (volumes or areas). \textquotesingle{}\textquotesingle{}\mbox{\hyperlink{structReservoirs}{Reservoirs}}\textquotesingle{}\textquotesingle{} such as pulmonary and mucous membrane exposures, will have nominal dimension 1 unit.
\item $ \mathbf{C} = (C_1, \ldots, C_n)$ are {\bfseries{ concentrations }} (pathogen units / unit area or volume). For an infector, this is {\bfseries{viral load}}, i.\+e., pathogen units per unit volume of resp. fluid, for an air zone, pathogen units per unit volume of air, for a surface, pathogen units per unit area, etc.
\item $ (m_{ij}) $ is the {\bfseries{ source matrix }}. E.\+g., infector $i$ breathing into an air zone $j$ with $m_{ij}$ being the {\bfseries{volume of resp.\+fluid exhaled by infector, per unit time}} and $C_i$ is the {\bfseries{viral load}} in pathogen units per volume. Another example, a susceptible person $j$ is exposed to air in zone $ i$ and $m_{ij}$ is their {\bfseries{pulmonary rate}}
\item $ (\beta_{ij}) $ is the {\bfseries{ mixing matrix }} whose terms are the volume rates of mixing between reservoirs. E.\+g., air mixing between zones and droplet deposition from air onto surfaces
\item $Q_i$ and $\gamma_i$ are {\bfseries{ sink terms }}, resp. rates of ventilation and pathogen decay from reservoir $i$.
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

I.\+e., \[ \frac{d\mathbf{C}}{dt} = D^{-1} X \mathbf{C} \]

where $D = \text{diag}(d_1, \ldots, d_n)$ and

\[ X_{ij} = \left\{ \begin{array}{rl} \beta_{ji} + m_{ji} &i \neq j\\ -\sum_{k \neq i} \beta_{ik} - Q_i - \gamma_i & i = j\\ \end{array} \right. \]
